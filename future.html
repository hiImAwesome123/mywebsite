DIY Fiberglass Swimming Pools

Save thousands by installing your own fiberglass pool — with professional excavation support.

Why Choose a Fiberglass Pool?

Fiberglass pools are one of the most durable, low‑maintenance, and cost‑effective pool options available. They arrive as a single molded shell, ready to be set into the ground — making them ideal for DIY installation with the right excavation and site prep.

The DIY Advantage

  • Save $10,000–$30,000 in labor costs
  • Much faster installation than concrete pools
  • No curing time — swim sooner
  • Smooth, non‑porous surface resists algae
  • Long‑lasting with minimal upkeep

What You Need to Get Started

Installing a fiberglass pool is absolutely doable with the right tools and preparation. Most DIY installers use:

  • Excavator or skid steer (we can help with this!)
  • Crushed stone or gravel base
  • Laser level for perfect grading
  • Plumbing supplies for returns and skimmer
  • Backfill material
  • Basic hand tools

Step‑by‑Step Installation Process

  1. Choose your fiberglass shell — size, shape, and features.
  2. Excavate the pool area — dig to manufacturer specifications.
  3. Set the gravel base — level and compact.
  4. Lower the pool shell — crane or equipment required.
  5. Level the shell — adjust until perfect.
  6. Plumb the system — returns, skimmer, pump.
  7. Backfill and fill with water — simultaneously for stability.
  8. Finish the patio — concrete, pavers, or decking.
